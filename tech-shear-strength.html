Slope Stabilization: Shear Strength at 60lbs/Acre

The Physics of Sliding

On a 2:1 slope (2 feet horizontal for every 1 foot vertical), gravity is constantly trying to pull the soil face downward. The only thing resisting this movement is the internal shear strength of the soil. When soil gets wet, its internal friction drops, and gravity wins—resulting in a landslide or slumping.

A hydroseeding binder's job is to increase the shear strength of the soil surface. But not all binders are created equal. A "glue" that is brittle will crack when the soil shifts. You need a material that is "pseudoplastic"—one that can stretch and deform without breaking.

High-Loading Protocol

For mild slopes (3:1 or 4:1), a light tacking rate of 30 lbs/acre is sufficient to hold straw mulch. However, for critical 2:1 or 1:1 slopes, we mandate a loading rate of 60 lbs/acre or more. At this concentration, the polymer chains of OHRA-BOND™ GREEN become dense enough to entangle with each other, forming a continuous, flexible web across the soil surface.

This web acts like a microscopic geogrid. Even if the soil underneath shifts slightly, the polymer matrix stretches to accommodate the movement, holding the mulch and seed in place. This "elastic recovery" is the defining characteristic of high-quality hydrocolloids vs. rigid starch or copolymer binders.
