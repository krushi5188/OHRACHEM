Soil Chemistry: Neutral pH Binders & Nutrient Uptake

Germination Shock

When a seed first cracks open, the emerging root hair (radicle) is incredibly sensitive to its chemical environment. Many synthetic soil binders and copolymers are highly acidic, with pH levels dropping as low as 4.0 or 5.0. When this acidic "glue" is in direct contact with the seed, it can chemically burn the root tip, stunting growth or killing the seedling entirely before it establishes.

The Buffer Zone

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is naturally buffered to a neutral pH range of 6.5 to 7.5. This mirrors the ideal pH range for most grasses and legumes used in erosion control. By maintaining a neutral environment immediately around the seed, we ensure optimal nutrient uptake.

Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium (N-P-K) are most available to plants at a neutral pH. If your binder acidifies the micro-climate around the seed, you can lock out these nutrients, wasting the expensive fertilizer you added to the tank. Neutral chemistry is not just about environmental safety; it is about maximizing the ROI of your seed and fertilizer investment.
