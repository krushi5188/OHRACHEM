Tank Maintenance: Post-Job Cleaning Protocols

The Concrete of Chemistry

Dried hydrocolloid is stronger than concrete. If you allow OHRA-BOND™ GREEN to dry inside your tank, agitator shaft, or pump casing, you will have a nightmare on your hands. Chipping away dried polymer can damage the protective epoxy coating of your tank, leading to rust and corrosion.

Daily Flush Protocol

Tank hygiene is not optional; it is mandatory. At the end of every spray day, follow this protocol:

  1. Drain: Spray out all remaining material. Do not leave 50 gallons in the sump.
  2. Fill: Add 50-100 gallons of fresh water.
  3. Circulate: Run the agitators and the recirculation (bypass) valve for 5 minutes. This scrubs the internal plumbing.
  4. Spray: Discharge this rinse water through the hose and nozzle to clear the lines.
If a crust has already formed, DO NOT chisel it. Hydrocolloids are reversible. Fill the tank with hot water and let it soak overnight. The polymer will re-hydrate and soften, allowing you to wash it out easily the next morning.

Keep it clean.
