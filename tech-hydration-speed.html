15-Second Delay: The Science of Dispersibility

The "Fish-Eye" Phenomenon

One of the biggest frustrations for hydroseeder operators is the formation of "fish-eyes" or "gel balls." This occurs when a highly active powder hits the water and the outer surface hydrates instantly, forming a waterproof gel seal around a core of dry powder. These lumps clog pumps, nozzles, and hoses, and they represent wasted material that never disperses into the slurry.

Standard raw guar gum is notorious for this. It is "too active" for most field mixing conditions, requiring slow, careful sifting to avoid lumping.

Surface Activation Technology

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN undergoes a proprietary surface treatment process during manufacturing. We lightly cross-link the surface of the particle with a dispersant that temporarily inhibits water absorption. When the powder hits the water, it does not swell immediately.

This creates a 15-second hydration delay. During these critical 15 seconds, the mechanical agitators in the tank can disperse the individual particles throughout the water column. Only after they are fully separated does the surface treatment dissolve, allowing the particle to swell rapidly to full viscosity. The result is a smooth, homogeneous slurry with zero lumps, achieved with zero effort.
