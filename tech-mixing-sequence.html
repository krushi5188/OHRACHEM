Best Practices: Agitation Order for Mulch & Binders

Molecular Competition

Hydroseeding is a battle for water. The mulch fiber acts as a sponge, trying to absorb water. The hydrocolloid tackifier acts as a super-absorbent polymer, also trying to grab water. If you mix them in the wrong order, you create "Molecular Competition."

If you load the mulch first, it will rapidly absorb the free water in the tank. When you add the tackifier powder afterwards, there is insufficient free water available to hydrate the polymer fully. The powder gets trapped in dry pockets within the mulch slurry, never dissolving, never swelling, and essentially doing nothing.

The Golden Rule of Sequencing

To maximize the yield of your materials, always follow this strict mixing sequence:

  1. Water: Fill tank to adequate level.
  2. Chemistry (OHRA-BOND): Add tackifier first. Allow it to fully hydrate and thicken the water into a "viscous soup."
  3. Seed & Fertilizer: Add amendments into the thickened water to suspend them.
  4. Mulch: Add fiber last. The thickened water will now coat the fiber rather than just soaking into it.
This ensures that 100% of the tackifier is active and coating the mulch fibers, creating the strongest possible matrix on the soil.
