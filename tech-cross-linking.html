Molecular Bonding: Cross-Linking with Soil Particles

Electrostatic Bridging

Erosion control is essentially a battle of physics and chemistry against gravity. Soil particles (clay and silt) typically carry a negative surface charge or a complex charge distribution depending on pH. To stabilize them, you need a "bridge."

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN contains long-chain anionic polysaccharides. While "like repels like," in the complex chemistry of soil water (which often contains divalent cations like Calcium or Magnesium), these polymer chains form "cationic bridges" between the soil particles. This process, known as flocculation, pulls fine dust and silt particles together into larger, heavier aggregates.

The Soil Crust

As the hydroseeding slurry dries, these polymer chains contract, tightening the bond between the mulch fibers and the soil aggregates. This forms a flexible, permeable crust. It is not a solid sheet like plastic; it is a microscopic web that allows air and water to penetrate while physically restraining the soil particles from detaching under the impact of raindrops.

This cross-linking effect is why high-quality hydrocolloids are so effective at dust control. They don't just wet the dust; they chemically bind the individual dust specks into a unified surface crust that resists wind shear.
