USLE Data: Reducing Soil Loss on Disturbed Sites

The Equation of Erosion

The Universal Soil Loss Equation (USLE) is the standard model used by civil engineers to predict soil loss. The formula is A = R x K x LS x C x P. As an erosion control specialist, the only variable you can significantly influence is "C" (Cover Management Factor).

Bare soil has a C-Factor of 1.0 (Maximum Erosion). Loose straw reduces this, but not enough for steep slopes. Crimped straw is better but inconsistent.

The C-Factor of Tackifiers

Applying OHRA-BOND™ GREEN at the recommended rate lowers the C-Factor dramatically, often below 0.05. This represents a 95% reduction in potential soil loss compared to bare ground. By chemically locking the mulch fibers to the soil, we prevent the "under-cutting" and rilling that typically initiates slope failure. Our data allows engineers to model their stormwater pollution prevention plans (SWPPP) with confidence, knowing the theoretical soil loss will stay well within EPA compliance limits.
