The Freeze/Thaw Test

Synthetic emulsions (latex or acrylic) are notorious for breaking down if they freeze. The ice crystals shear the emulsion droplets, separating the water from the solid rubber. Once thawed, you have a bucket of cottage cheese that cannot be sprayed.

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is supplied as a dry powder. Dry powder cannot freeze. But what about the mixed slurry? We tested hydrated slurry samples through 5 freeze/thaw cycles (-10°F to 70°F).

Viscosity Retention

Cycle 1: 100% Viscosity Retention.
Cycle 5: 98% Viscosity Retention.
The hydrocolloid gel structure is "thermally reversible." It freezes solid, but upon thawing, the polymer chains re-hydrate and regain their full thickening power. This means a tank left to freeze overnight is not a total loss. You can thaw it out and spray it the next day with confidence.

High Heat Performance

Conversely, at high temperatures (100°F+), many liquid polymers "thin out" drastically, losing their ability to hold mulch in suspension. OHRA-BOND maintains a stable viscosity curve even in extreme heat, ensuring consistent application rates in the hottest deserts.
