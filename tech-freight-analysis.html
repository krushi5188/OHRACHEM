COGS Analysis: Concentrate vs. Pre-Mixed Blocks (Freight Savings)

The Binder-to-Carrier Ratio

In the global erosion control market, logistics often define the margin. The industry standard "Mulch Block" or "BFM (Bonded Fiber Matrix)" typically consists of a 50lb (22.7kg) bale containing roughly 45lbs of wood fiber or paper mulch and only 5lbs of actual chemical binder. This results in a 90% dead weight shipping ratio.

When you import pre-mixed blocks, you are effectively paying international freight rates to ship wood fiber that is likely available locally in your target market. Wood fiber is a high-volume, low-value commodity. Shipping it across oceans in 40ft containers drastically inflates the landed cost per acre of the actual active ingredient: the tackifier.

The Math of High-Purity Concentrates

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is a 95% active hydrocolloid concentrate. A single 50lb bag of our product contains the equivalent binding power of approximately 10-15 pallets of standard pre-mixed mulch blocks. By decoupling the chemistry from the carrier (mulch), contractors can source fiber locally—saving thousands in freight—and dose the binder precisely on-site.

For international distributors, this shifts the business model from moving high-volume logistics to high-value chemistry. A single 20ft container of OHRA-BOND can treat thousands of acres, whereas a container of BFM blocks might only cover 20-30 acres. The efficiency gains in Class 50 freight rates are undeniable.

Stop Shipping Dead Weight

You add the bulk (water and mulch) at the job site, where it costs nothing to transport.
