PAM (Polyacrylamide) vs. Organic Hydrocolloids

The Synthetic Dilemma

Polyacrylamide (PAM) has been a popular soil stabilizer for decades due to its low cost and high flocculating power. However, PAM is a synthetic polymer often derived from petroleum feedstocks. While "anionic" PAM is generally considered safe, it can still contain trace amounts of unreacted acrylamide monomer, a known neurotoxin and carcinogen.

Because of this risk, many municipalities and environmental agencies are restricting the use of PAM, especially near sensitive watersheds, food crops, or residential areas. The liability of introducing a synthetic plastic-like polymer into the soil ecosystem is becoming a risk that many engineers are no longer willing to take.

The Organic Alternative

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is 100% vegetable origin. It is a polysaccharide gum extracted from the endosperm of the Plantago ovata or similar species. It contains zero synthetic polymers, zero acrylamide, and zero petroleum derivatives. It is completely non-toxic and biodegradable.

Despite being organic, its performance rivals or exceeds that of PAM. Our micro-activation process gives it the same long-chain cross-linking ability to bind soil particles (flocculation) without the environmental baggage. For projects targeting LEED certification or strict EPA compliance, organic hydrocolloids are the only future-proof choice.
