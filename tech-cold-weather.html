Winter Hydroseeding: Hydration in <50°F Water

Thermal Kinetics

Chemistry slows down in the cold. The hydration of a hydrocolloid is a kinetic process where water molecules penetrate the polymer chain to "unfold" it. In summer (70°F+ water), OHRA-BOND™ GREEN reaches 90% of its maximum viscosity within 10 minutes. However, in winter conditions where hydrant water can be 40°F or colder, this reaction slows significantly.

The Viscosity Lag

If you mix for only 10 minutes in freezing water, you may only achieve 60% of the potential viscosity. The slurry will look thin. If you spray immediately, you are wasting performance potential. Worse, the binder will continue to hydrate after it hits the ground, but it won't have the mechanical mixing energy to reach its full swell volume.

Winter Protocol

To combat the cold, simply add time. In water temperatures below 50°F, we recommend extending the mixing time by 5 to 10 minutes.
Tip: Recirculate the slurry through the pump (bypass loop) during this extra time. The mechanical shear from the pump adds kinetic energy and friction, actually warming the water slightly and accelerating hydration. Wait until the slurry looks "ropey" and thick before opening the gun.
