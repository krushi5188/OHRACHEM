Wildfire Restoration: Stabilizing Hydrophobic Ash Layers

The Ash Slide Risk

Post-wildfire landscapes present a unique danger. The intense heat of the fire vaporizes organic compounds in the soil, which then re-condense as a waxy, water-repellent (hydrophobic) layer just below the surface. When rain falls on this layer, it cannot infiltrate. Instead, it lubricates the layer of loose ash on top, creating an instant mudslide or debris flow.

Surfactant Properties

Standard water alone will just bead up and roll off this hydrophobic ash. To stabilize it, you need a binder that also acts as a wetting agent. OHRA-BOND™ GREEN lowers the surface tension of the water, allowing the slurry to penetrate the ash layer rather than sitting on top of it.

By binding the loose ash particles into a cohesive crust and adhering them to the underlying mineral soil, we can "lock" the slope in place before the winter rains arrive. This emergency stabilization buys time for the dormant seed bank (or applied seed) to sprout and take over the job of erosion control.
