Nozzle Selection Guide: Spray Patterns for Slurries

The Fan Nozzle Mistake

Many operators default to using a "Fan Nozzle" (flat spray tip) because it paints a pretty, wide picture. While good for watering dust, fan nozzles have small orifice geometries that create extreme shear forces. When you force a long-chain polymer through a tiny slit at high pressure, you risk "mechanical shear degradation"—literally chopping the molecular chains in half.

This degrades the viscosity of the product right at the exit point. You might have mixed a perfect slurry in the tank, but you are spraying a thinner product onto the soil.

Preserve the Chain

For high-viscosity slurries containing OHRA-BOND™ GREEN, we recommend using a large-bore "Wide-Stream" or "Boom" nozzle. These tips have a larger orifice that reduces exit shear, preserving the integrity of the polymer chain. This allows the material to exit as a heavy, cohesive droplet rather than a fine mist.

Heavy droplets carry more kinetic energy (momentum), allowing them to penetrate the straw mulch or soil surface deeper, ensuring better bonding. Mist just sits on top. Use the stream nozzle to "inject" the binder into the matrix.
