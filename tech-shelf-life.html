Stability Report: Powder vs. Liquid Emulsions in High Heat

The Thermodynamics of Shipping

Global logistics involves extreme thermal cycling. A standard shipping container crossing the equator can reach internal temperatures exceeding 140°F (60°C). For liquid soil binders—typically emulsions of polymers in water—this environment is catastrophic. Emulsions rely on chemical surfactants to keep oil and water phases mixed. At sustained high temperatures, these surfactants degrade, leading to "stratification" where the polymer separates into a hard sludge at the bottom of the tote.

Once an emulsion stratifies, it is often chemically impossible to re-mix, rendering the product useless upon arrival. This is a common and expensive failure mode for liquid PAM or copolymer blends shipped internationally.

Inert Powder Stability

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is an inert, dry powder. It contains zero water content. Without water, there is no medium for bacterial growth, fermentation, or phase separation. In our high-heat stability tests, our hydrocolloid powder showed no degradation in viscosity or swell volume after being subjected to 150°F (65°C) for 30 days.

5-Year Shelf Life

Because the product is biologically dormant until it contacts water, its shelf life is dictated only by moisture control. Packaged in our multi-wall, moisture-resistant bags, OHRA-BOND maintains its full efficacy for up to 5 years. This allows distributors to buy in bulk without fear of "expiry dates" forcing them to discard unsold inventory. For remote project sites in deserts or tropics, this thermal stability is not just a feature; it is a requirement.
