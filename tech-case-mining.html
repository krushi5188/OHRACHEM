Case Study: Mining Dust Suppression

The Challenge

Project: Active Copper Mine, Chile.
Site: 500-acre dry tailings pond.
Problem: High winds were carrying PM10 particulate dust into a nearby village. The mine was facing a regulatory shutdown due to air quality violations.

The Solution

The mine deployed a fleet of water trucks equipped with monitor cannons. They mixed OHRA-BOND™ GREEN at a rate of 15 lbs/acre into the water. No mulch was used; just water and binder.

The Result

The application created an immediate surface crust. Within 24 hours, opacity readings at the fence line dropped by 95%. The crust remained effective for 21 days despite high winds, allowing the mine to continue operations without interruption.
Advantage: Unlike Magnesium Chloride (salt), the organic binder did not corrode the expensive haul trucks driving over the treated areas.
Conclusion: Chemical crusting is a scalable solution for industrial dust control.
