Case Study: Golf Course Bunker Stabilization

The Challenge

Project: Private Country Club, Arizona.
Feature: Steep-faced "pot bunkers" with 60-degree sand faces.
Problem: After every irrigation cycle or wind storm, the sand would slide down the face, requiring daily manual labor to rake back up.

The Solution

The Superintendent implemented a micro-dosing protocol. Using a backpack sprayer (or small tank skid), the maintenance crew applied OHRA-BOND™ GREEN at a very low rate (5 lbs/acre equivalent) directly onto the sand faces after raking.

The Result

The binder created an invisible, permeable crust on the sand surface. It was strong enough to resist wind scour and irrigation runoff, keeping the steep faces pristine. Yet, it was brittle enough that a golf club could easily break through it during play (preserving the "hazard" nature of the bunker).
ROI: Bunker maintenance labor was reduced by 70%. The bunkers held their shape for weeks between touch-ups.
Conclusion: Precision chemistry solves aesthetic problems.
