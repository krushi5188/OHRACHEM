2:1 Slopes: Application Protocols for Verticality

The Gravity Problem

Spraying a 2:1 slope (or steeper) presents a unique physics problem. If you apply the full weight of the slurry in a single pass, the sheer mass of the water-laden mulch can overcome the internal friction of the soil surface, causing the entire mat to "slump" or slide down the hill before it dries. This creates bare spots at the top and a pile of mush at the toe of the slope.

The Shadowing Technique

To lock a steep slope, you must attack it from two angles. This is called "Shadowing."
Pass 1: Spray from the bottom up, or from left to right. This coats the "front" faces of the soil clods and furrows.
Pass 2: Spray from the top down, or from right to left. This coats the "back" faces (the shadows) that were missed in the first pass.

This multi-directional application ensures that the mulch fibers wrap around soil particles rather than just sitting on top of them. For extreme slopes (>1:1), consider a two-stage application: apply 50% of the material, let it dry for 2 hours to become "tacky," and then apply the second 50%. This builds a laminated crust that defies gravity.

Conquer the vertical.

Use shadowing techniques for complete coverage.
