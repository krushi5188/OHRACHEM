Groundwater Safety: Leaching Analysis

The Migration Myth

A common concern with chemical soil amendments is "leaching"—the idea that the chemical will dissolve in rain, percolate through the soil profile, and contaminate the groundwater table below. For small-molecule fertilizers (Nitrates), this is a valid concern.

However, hydrocolloids function on a different physical scale. The polymer chains in OHRA-BOND™ GREEN are enormous macromolecules with high molecular weights (often exceeding 1 million Daltons). Physically, these chains are simply too large to migrate through the pore spaces of compacted subsoil. They are physically strained out by the soil matrix within the top few millimeters.

Surface-Active Only

Because of this molecular geometry, OHRA-BOND remains strictly a surface treatment. It creates a crust on top of the soil but does not penetrate deep into the profile. It binds the surface particles to prevent erosion but poses virtually zero risk to aquifers or wells located below the treatment zone. It stays where you put it until it biodegrades.
