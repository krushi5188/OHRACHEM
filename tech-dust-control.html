Fugitive Dust: Surface Crusting Ratios

The Weekend Problem

On active construction sites or mine tailings ponds, fugitive dust is a major compliance liability. Often, you don't need to vegetate the area (as it will be worked on next week), but you need to "lock it down" over the weekend to prevent wind-blown PM10 particulate pollution.

Traditional watering trucks are a temporary fix; the water evaporates in hours. Magnesium Chloride works but is corrosive to equipment. The modern solution is a "Surface Tension Crust."

Mulch-Free Application

For dust control, you do not need fiber mulch. You can apply OHRA-BOND™ GREEN straight, mixed only with water. The goal is to lightly coat the surface particles, gluing them together into a thin, permeable crust.

Recommended Rate: 15-20 lbs per acre.
Mix this low dose into a full tank of water and spray it rapidly over the target area. The hydrocolloid will bind the fine dust particles into heavier aggregates that the wind cannot lift. This crust remains effective for weeks, yet is easily broken up by bulldozers when work resumes, leaving only biodegradable organic residue in the soil.
