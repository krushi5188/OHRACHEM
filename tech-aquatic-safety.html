Toxicity Report: Safety for Aquatic Life

The LC50 Standard

When working near waterways, wetlands, or storm drains, the toxicity of your runoff is a primary concern. The standard metric for aquatic safety is "LC50" (Lethal Concentration 50%), which measures the concentration of a substance required to kill 50% of a test population (usually Rainbow Trout or Daphnia Magna) within 96 hours.

For a chemical to be considered "Practically Non-Toxic" by the EPA, it must have an LC50 greater than 100 mg/L. The higher the number, the safer the product.

Practical Non-Toxicity

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN consistently tests with an LC50 > 1,000 mg/L (and often > 3,000 mg/L depending on the species). This is orders of magnitude safer than the EPA threshold. In practical terms, you could dump a bag of our binder directly into a pond (we don't recommend it, but you could) without causing a fish kill event.

This safety profile allows contractors to use OHRA-BOND confidently in riparian zones, stream bank restorations, and sensitive wetland buffers where synthetic flocculants or anionic PAMs might be restricted.
