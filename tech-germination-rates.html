Growth Data: Porous Matrices vs. Hard Crusts

The "Glass Ceiling" Effect

Erosion control is a balancing act. You need a surface strong enough to stop rain impact, but weak enough to let a delicate seedling push through. Some binders, particularly certain copolymers and hard-setting cements, create a "cap" that is impenetrable to emerging vegetation. We call this the "Glass Ceiling Effect." The seed germinates, hits the hard crust, and dies of exhaustion before it can emerge.

The Porous Lattice

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN dries into a "microporous lattice." It functions more like a microscopic net than a solid sheet of plastic. This structure allows free exchange of Oxygen and CO2—critical for root respiration. It also allows the coleoptile (the first shoot of the grass) to easily penetrate the surface.

In our comparative field trials, plots treated with our porous hydrocolloid showed a 15% higher germination rate compared to plots treated with hard-crusting guar or PAM blends. It turns out that helping the seed breathe is just as important as holding the soil.
