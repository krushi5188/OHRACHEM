Time-Motion Study: Tank Loading Speeds

The Cost of Downtime

In hydroseeding, the machine only makes money when it is spraying. Any time spent at the hydrant loading water or materials is lost revenue. We conducted a time-motion study comparing the loading times for a standard 3,000-gallon hydroseeder using two different material systems: Pre-Mixed Mulch Blocks and the OHRA-BOND™ Concentrate System.

Scenario A: Pre-Mixed Blocks

To achieve the required tackifier loading for a steep slope using pre-mixed blocks, the crew had to load 60 bales of mulch. Each bale requires manual lifting, cutting of plastic wrap, and feeding into the shredder box.
Total Load Time: 35 minutes (2 laborers).
Physical Strain: High (repetitive lifting of 50lb bales).

Scenario B: OHRA-BOND Concentrate

Using the OHRA system, the crew loaded locally sourced loose mulch (bulk loaded via hopper or blown in) and added the tackifier separately. The requirement was 3 bags (150 lbs) of OHRA-BOND.
Total Load Time (Binder): 90 seconds.
Total Load Time (Mulch): 10 minutes (using bulk methods).
Total Savings: ~23 minutes per tank.

The Multiplier Effect

Saving 23 minutes per load allows a crew to spray one extra tank per day. Over the course of a week, this efficiency gain translates to a 20% increase in total billable acreage without increasing payroll or equipment hours. Fast-wetting chemistry is not just about science; it's about speed.
