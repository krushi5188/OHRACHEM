Case Study: Residential Development Erosion

The Challenge

Project: 50-Lot Subdivision, Georgia.
Problem: The developer was issued a "Stop Work" order after a sediment discharge event. The local inspector required immediate stabilization of all disturbed soil (20 acres) before work could resume.
Constraint: Installing 20 acres of silt fence and erosion blankets would take a week and cost $50,000.

The Solution

The contractor proposed "Hydro-Tacking." They spread wheat straw over the site at 2 tons/acre and then sprayed it with OHRA-BOND™ GREEN at 40 lbs/acre. This effectively "glued" the straw to the ground.

The Result

The application was completed in 2 days using a single hydroseeder truck. The inspector visited on Day 3, walked the site, and lifted the Stop Work order.
Cost Savings: The developer saved approximately $35,000 compared to the cost of blankets and silt fence. The mulch eventually decomposed, adding organic matter to the lawn areas for the future homeowners.
Conclusion: Speed of application is a critical factor in regulatory compliance.
