Architect's Guide: How to Spec High-Purity Hydrocolloids

Stop Specifying "Guar"

Writing a specification that says "Guar Gum" is like specifying "Wood" for a house frame. You might get oak, or you might get rotten pine. It leaves the door open for contractors to use the cheapest agricultural-grade powder they can find, leading to project failure.

The Performance Spec

To ensure your project gets built with high-quality materials, you must write a "Performance Specification" based on measurable data. We recommend the following language for Section 32 92 19 (Seeding):
"Tackifier shall be a high-purity vegetable hydrocolloid. It shall be non-toxic, 100% biodegradable, and free of synthetic polymers or acrylamides. It must meet the following minimum parameters:
- Viscosity (1% Sol): >3,000 cps
- Swell Volume: >35 ml/g
- Mesh Size: >95% passing 90 mesh"

Enforcement

Require submittals. Ask for a Certificate of Analysis (COA) for the specific batch delivered to the site. If the COA doesn't show a viscosity curve, reject it. By tightening your spec, you force the industry to raise its standards.
