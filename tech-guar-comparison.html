Lab Comparison: OHRA-BOND vs. Generic Guar

Generic Guar is for Ice Cream

The vast majority of the world's guar gum supply is produced for the food and fracking industries. "Agricultural Grade" guar is often the lowest quality byproduct—the floor sweepings of the industry. It varies wildly in viscosity, mesh size, and purity from batch to batch.

More critically, raw guar is highly susceptible to biological degradation. In a warm hydroseeding tank, bacteria begin to ferment the sugar chains within hours. If you leave a load of generic guar in the tank overnight, you will likely return to a foul-smelling, watery soup with zero tackifying power. The viscosity crash is rapid and irreversible.

Standardized for Dirt

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is not generic. It is a standardized hydrocolloid engineered specifically for soil stabilization. We include a benign preservative package that inhibits bacterial fermentation for up to 7 days. This means you can mix a tank on Friday, leave it over the weekend, and spray it on Monday with no loss of performance.

Furthermore, our mesh size (98% passing 90 mesh) is optimized for rapid hydration without clumping. Generic guar is often too coarse (slow to hydrate) or too fine (instant fish-eyes). We control the particle size distribution to ensure consistent field performance, every single time.
