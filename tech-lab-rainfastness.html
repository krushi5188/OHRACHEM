CASE STUDIES & DATA

Lab Data: 4-Hour Cure Time Analysis

The Methodology

We subjected two identical soil test plots (3:1 slope, sandy loam) to a simulated rainfall event.
Plot A: Treated with Starch-Based Copolymer.
Plot B: Treated with OHRA-BOND™ GREEN (Hydrocolloid).
Both were allowed to cure for exactly 4 hours at 70°F before the rain began. The "rain" was calibrated to 2 inches per hour (severe storm intensity).

The Data

Plot A (Starch): Failed within 15 minutes. The starch re-solubilized and washed off as a milky white runoff. Soil loss was significant (turbidity > 1000 NTU).
Plot B (OHRA): Held for the full 60-minute duration. The hydrocolloid swelled and became tacky, locking the soil particles together. Runoff was clear (turbidity < 50 NTU).

The Conclusion

Chemical "Cure Time" is critical. OHRA-BOND does not need days to dry; it needs only hours to set. This rapid activation makes it the superior choice for climates with frequent afternoon thunderstorms.
