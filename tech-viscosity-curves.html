Defining Centipoise (cps)

Viscosity is the measure of a fluid's resistance to flow. In the erosion control industry, it is the primary indicator of a binder's ability to hold mulch and seed in suspension and stick to a slope. We measure this in centipoise (cps). Water is 1 cps. Honey is ~10,000 cps. Standard agricultural guar gum typically measures between 2,200 and 2,800 cps in a 1% solution.

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is engineered to achieve a minimum viscosity of 3250 cps (Brookfield Viscometer, Spindle 4, 20 RPM). This significant increase over generic gums is not accidental; it is the result of specific micro-milling and activation processes that expose more hydration sites on the polysaccharide chain.

The "Fluid Curtain" Effect

Why does high viscosity matter? When a hydroseeding slurry exits the nozzle, it must maintain a cohesive stream (the "fluid curtain") to ensure uniform coverage. Low-viscosity slurries tend to atomize too much, causing "splash-out" when they hit the soil. This splash-out displaces the very soil particles you are trying to stabilize.

A high-viscosity binder like OHRA-BOND creates a thick, cohesive matrix that absorbs the kinetic energy of the impact. Instead of splashing, it "splats" and sticks, instantly locking the mulch fibers to the soil surface. This is critical for 2:1 slopes where gravity is working against you from the moment the material leaves the hose.
