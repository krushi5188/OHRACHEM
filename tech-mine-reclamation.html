Mine Reclamation: Phytostabilization of Tailings

The Toxic Dust Hazard

Mine tailings often contain heavy metals (Lead, Arsenic, Cadmium) or generate acidic drainage (pyrite oxidation). If these tailings dry out and become airborne dust, they pose a severe health risk to surrounding communities. The traditional solution is to cap them with feet of topsoil—an expensive and logistically difficult process.

Vegetative Capping

"Phytostabilization" is the use of plants to lock these contaminants in place. The roots hold the soil, and the foliage prevents wind erosion. However, establishing plants on toxic, acidic, sterile tailings is incredibly difficult.

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is instrumental in this process. Its high pH buffering capacity helps neutralize micro-sites of acidity around the seed. Its water-holding capacity creates a survival zone for seedlings in the harsh, rocky substrate. By gluing the toxic dust down and nursing the vegetation to life, we transform a hazardous waste site into a stable, green landscape without the massive cost of importing topsoil.
