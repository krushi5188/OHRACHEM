Buying Strategy: Seasonal vs. Just-In-Time

The Agricultural Cycle

Unlike synthetic polymers which are produced on demand in chemical plants, vegetable hydrocolloids (guar, psyllium, starch) are agricultural products. Their pricing is dictated by harvest yields, monsoon patterns in India/Pakistan, and global demand. Typically, prices are lowest immediately following the harvest (Winter/Early Spring) and peak during the summer growing season when supply tightens.

Contractors who buy "Just-In-Time" during the peak of construction season (Summer) are often purchasing at the top of the market. They are subject to spot pricing volatility that can swing 20-30% in a single month.

Winter Stockpiling Strategy

Because OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is a dry powder with a 5-year shelf life, it is the perfect candidate for strategic stockpiling. Large distributors and wise contractors procure their annual requirements during the winter months, locking in lower rates. The high density of the product (see our article on Warehouse Logistics) makes storage cheap.

OHRA Chem offers forward-contracting options for export clients. By committing to volume in Q1, you insulate your business from Q3 price spikes. This "Supply Chain Elasticity" allows you to bid on large government tenders with fixed material costs, protecting your margin against market inflation.
