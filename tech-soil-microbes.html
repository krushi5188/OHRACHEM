Regenerative Ag: Binders as Carbon Sources

The Soil Biome

Dead soil doesn't support life. On construction sites, the topsoil has often been stripped away, leaving a sterile mineral subsoil devoid of organic matter and microbial life. To establish long-term vegetation, you need to "wake up" the biology.

Synthetic binders (plastics) are inert; they offer nothing to the soil food web. However, OHRA-BOND™ GREEN is essentially a complex sugar (polysaccharide). To a soil microbe (bacteria or fungi), our binder is lunch.

The Prebiotic Effect

As the hydrocolloid degrades, it releases carbon that acts as a "prebiotic" for beneficial soil organisms. This spike in microbial activity accelerates the breakdown of mulch fibers into humus, releasing nutrients and improving soil structure (tilth). We are not just gluing the soil together; we are kickstarting the regenerative process that turns sterile dirt into living soil. This biological integration is the key to sustainable, self-healing landscapes.
