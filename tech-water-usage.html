Hydraulic Efficiency: Maximizing Coverage Per Tank

The Slurry Rheology Paradox

Operators often believe that adding more mulch to a tank requires adding more water to keep it pumpable. This limits the "payload" of fiber per tank, forcing the machine to return to the hydrant more frequently. However, with the right chemistry, you can actually pump thicker slurries with less water.

Lubricity of Hydrocolloids

OHRA-BOND™ GREEN acts as a potent lubricant. Its pseudoplastic nature means that under the high shear of the pump impeller, its viscosity drops, reducing friction. This "shear-thinning" behavior allows the pump to move a slurry with a much higher solids content (mulch density) than would be possible with water alone.

By increasing the lubricity of the mix, you can load up to 15-20% more mulch per tank without clogging lines or cavitation. This effectively increases the square footage coverage of every tank load.

Water Conservation

In arid regions or sites where water must be trucked in, water is the most expensive component of the mix. By optimizing the mulch-to-water ratio using high-performance tackifiers, you reduce the total gallons of water required per acre of application. This not only saves water costs but also reduces the fuel burned hauling water tankers to the site.
